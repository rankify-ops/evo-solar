FAQS

Why do I need an inverter?

While solar panels collect the sunlight and turn it into energy, it’s the inverter that converts energy into usable power for your home. Inverters also monitor the solar panel’s performance and safety, making it an important component of your solar power system.

What’s the difference between a string inverter and a micro-inverter?

String inverters function by connecting solar panels together using ‘strings’ that are then linked to a single inverter. In contrast, micro-inverters pair each panel with its own individual inverter, allowing you to monitor individual solar panels in your system, making it easier to identify and rectify faults.

In general, micro-inverters are a safer system and are more efficient. They are a solid state device with no high voltage direct current (DC), giving them a much stronger lifespan than traditional string inverters.

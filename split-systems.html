Heating & Cooling

Year-Round Comfort

Split system air conditioners provide both heating and cooling in a single, efficient unit. Modern inverter technology adjusts the compressor speed to maintain your desired temperature, using only the energy needed rather than constantly cycling on and off. This means lower energy bills and a more comfortable home.

With a split system, you get quiet operation, precise temperature control, and the flexibility to heat or cool individual rooms as needed.

  • Both heating and cooling in one unit
  • Inverter technology for energy efficiency
  • Quiet operation for bedrooms and living areas
  • Precise temperature control room by room

Energy Efficiency & Solar Integration

When paired with a solar power system, split system air conditioners can run almost entirely on free energy from the sun during peak daytime hours. This is when cooling demand is highest, making solar and split systems a natural pairing for Australian homes.

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about split system air conditioners.

What size split system do I need?

The right size depends on the room dimensions, ceiling height, insulation, window placement, and climate. As a general guide, a 2.5kW unit suits a small bedroom, 5kW covers a standard living room, and 7–8kW is needed for larger open-plan areas. Our team will assess your space and recommend the correct size to ensure efficient performance.

Can a split system both heat and cool?

Yes, all modern split system air conditioners are reverse-cycle, meaning they provide both heating and cooling. They use a heat pump mechanism to transfer heat into your home during winter and out of your home during summer, making them one of the most energy-efficient ways to manage your home's temperature year-round.

How much does it cost to run a split system?

Running costs depend on the unit's capacity, energy star rating, how often you use it, and your electricity tariff. A modern inverter split system is significantly cheaper to run than older fixed-speed models or electric heaters. When paired with solar panels, your daytime running costs can be close to zero.

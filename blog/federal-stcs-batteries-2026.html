Federal STCs Now Available for Home Batteries — Up to $6,600 Off

Published 20 June 2026

If you've been thinking about adding a battery to your solar system, there's never been a better time. The federal government has officially expanded Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) to include home battery storage systems, meaning eligible households across Geelong, the Surf Coast, and all of Australia can now receive an upfront discount of up to $6,600 when installing a battery. This is a significant shift in how battery storage is incentivised in Australia, and it's already driving a surge in enquiries here at Evo Solar.

What Are STCs and How Do They Work?

STCs have been the backbone of solar panel affordability in Australia for over a decade. When you install an eligible renewable energy system, you earn a certain number of certificates based on the system size and how much clean energy it will produce over its lifetime. These certificates have a tradeable value, and in practice your installer applies the discount directly to your invoice — so you see the savings upfront without any paperwork or waiting. One STC is currently valued at around $40, and a typical home battery system can generate well over 100 certificates depending on its capacity and your location.

How Much Can You Actually Save?

The exact discount depends on the size of your battery and your postcode zone. For a standard 10kWh battery installed in the Geelong region, you can expect to receive approximately $4,000 to $5,000 in STC value. Larger systems like a 13.5kWh Tesla Powerwall or a 15kWh BYD unit can push savings closer to the $6,000–$6,600 mark. The discount is applied at the point of sale by your CEC-accredited installer — there's no separate application process, no voucher codes, and no waiting list. Your installer handles the STC paperwork and passes the savings directly to you.

What Happened to the Solar Victoria Battery Rebate?

Many Victorians will remember the Solar Victoria battery rebate, which offered up to $2,950 off a battery installation. That program has now closed to new applicants as funding was fully allocated. The good news is that the new federal STC eligibility for batteries actually provides a larger discount in most cases — often double what the old state rebate offered. Unlike the Victorian program, there are no income caps, no postcode restrictions within Australia, and no limited release dates to compete for. As long as your battery and installer meet the Clean Energy Council requirements, you're eligible.

Why Add a Battery Now?

Beyond the financial incentive, batteries make more practical sense than ever for Geelong and Surf Coast homeowners. Electricity prices continue to climb, feed-in tariffs have dropped significantly, and time-of-use billing means your power costs the most in the evening — exactly when your solar panels stop producing. A battery lets you store your daytime solar generation and use it at night, effectively shielding you from peak-rate electricity. Paired with backup capability, you also get protection during the power outages that can affect the Bellarine and coastal areas during storm season.

Energy-Efficient Hot Water

Heat Pump Hot Water Systems

Heat pump hot water systems work by extracting heat from the surrounding air and transferring it to water, making them up to three times more energy-efficient than conventional electric hot water systems. This means significant savings on your energy bills while reducing your carbon footprint.

By pairing a heat pump with your solar power system, you can heat your water using free energy from the sun, further maximising your savings and minimising your reliance on the grid.

  • Up to 80% reduction in hot water energy costs
  • Environmentally friendly — lower carbon emissions
  • Government rebates available for eligible households
  • Pairs perfectly with solar power systems

FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about heat pumps answered by our experts.

How does a heat pump hot water system work?

A heat pump works like a refrigerator in reverse. It extracts heat energy from the surrounding air and uses it to heat water. Even in cooler weather, there is enough ambient heat in the air for the system to operate efficiently. This makes heat pumps up to three times more efficient than traditional electric hot water systems.

How much can I save with a heat pump?

Most households can reduce their hot water energy costs by up to 80% by switching to a heat pump system. The exact savings depend on your current system, household size, and hot water usage. When combined with government rebates that can reduce the upfront cost significantly, the payback period is typically very short.

Is there a government rebate on heat pumps?

Yes, the Victorian Government offers substantial rebates on heat pump hot water systems for eligible households. Federal incentives through Small-scale Technology Certificates (STCs) also apply. Visit Geelong Heat Pumps for the latest rebate information and eligibility criteria.
